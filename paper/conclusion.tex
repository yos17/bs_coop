\section{Conclusion}

This repository studies localized distributed beamforming for cooperative multi-cell downlink systems with limited signaling. The proposed LDBPA iteration combines local beamformer optimization, power dual updates, and neighborhood interference pricing. Its key feature is a tunable cooperation radius that interpolates between non-cooperative transmission and global coordination. Under spatial decay, the paper shows that the localized optimum approaches the globally coordinated optimum as the neighborhood expands. The accompanying code reproduces the main numerical trends and provides a baseline for future extensions, including richer channel models, per-antenna constraints, and random spatial deployments.
